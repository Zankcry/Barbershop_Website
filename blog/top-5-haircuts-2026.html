Top 5 Haircuts Trending in 2026

From textured French crops to modern mullets, discover the hottest haircut trends dominating barbershops in 2026. Find out which style suits you best.

J

James

Expert Barber at Charlie's Barber & Salon

1. The Textured French Crop

The textured French crop continues to dominate in 2026, but with a modern twist. This year's version features heavier texturing on top with a slightly longer fringe that can be styled forward or swept to the side. Paired with a skin fade or low taper on the sides, it creates a sharp contrast that works for virtually any face shape. The beauty of the French crop is its versatility, it looks just as polished in a boardroom as it does on a night out. To nail this look, ask your barber for choppy layers on top with a disconnected fade for maximum impact. A matte clay or light-hold paste is all you need to style it daily, making it one of the most low-maintenance trendy cuts available right now.

2. The Modern Mullet

Love it or hate it, the mullet is here to stay, and 2026's version is more refined than ever. The modern mullet blends a textured, voluminous top with a tapered back that flows naturally instead of the abrupt 'business in the front, party in the back' of decades past. Many clients are pairing it with a mid or high fade on the sides for a cleaner, more contemporary look. It's a bold statement that shows personality and confidence. This style works especially well with wavy or curly hair, adding natural movement and dimension. If you love this wild aesthetic, you should also check out the Japanese Wolf Cut, a closely related style rooted in J-style barbering. The key to pulling off the modern mullet is having your barber texturize the back so it flows into the longer section seamlessly rather than looking like an afterthought.

3. The Burst Fade

The burst fade has exploded in popularity this year, thanks to its eye-catching silhouette. Unlike a traditional fade that follows a straight line, the burst fade radiates outward around the ear in a semicircular pattern, creating a sun-burst effect. It pairs beautifully with curly tops, mohawks, or even a drop fade combination. The burst fade is perfect for anyone wanting to stand out without going too extreme. It requires a skilled barber to execute properly, the blending around the ear has to be seamless for the best results. Many clients are adding a hard part line above the fade for extra definition, and it's quickly becoming one of our most-requested fades here at Charlie's.

4. Curtain Bangs with Middle Part

The curtain bangs trend has evolved in 2026 into a more structured, intentional style. Hair is grown out to medium length on top and parted down the middle, with the sides framing the face like curtains. The sides can be kept at a natural length or cleaned up with a subtle taper for a more polished look. This style has a relaxed, effortless vibe that appeals to younger generations while still looking put-together. It works best with straight to slightly wavy hair and requires minimal product, just a light texturizing spray or cream to add separation. Popularized by K-drama actors and trending hard on social media, curtain bangs share the same aesthetic DNA as Japanese haircuts like the Two-Block Cut, letting you show off face-framing volume without heavy commitment.

5. The Buzz Cut with Hard Part or Design

The classic buzz cut gets a 2026 upgrade with the addition of razor-sharp hard parts and creative line designs. This low-maintenance style is perfect for the hot Philippine climate and active lifestyles. A single hard part line adds instant structure, while more adventurous clients are opting for geometric patterns or subtle hair tattoo designs. The buzz cut with a design is quick to style, literally zero effort in the morning, looks clean for weeks, and makes a strong statement. It's one of the most requested styles at our shop this year. Best of all, it suits every face shape and works on all hair types. Pair it with a clean beard lineup and you've got a look that turns heads with minimal upkeep.

Final Thoughts

Whether you're looking for something low-maintenance like a buzz cut or want to make a bold statement with a modern mullet, 2026 has a haircut for every personality. The key trend across all styles this year is texture and contrast, combining clean fades with natural movement on top. Don't be afraid to bring reference photos to your next barber visit; a great barber can adapt any trend to your unique hair type and face shape.
